## Why this blog exists

This is the first real marker for Dev with Min.

I want this blog to become a durable record of my developer experience: what I
build, what breaks, what I learn, what I change my mind about, and what tools or
ideas become part of my everyday work.

<Note title="Personal first">
  This is not meant to be a polished publication machine. It is a personal
  developer journal that can hold rough notes, practical logs, and deeper
  technical thinking in the same place.
</Note>

## What I plan to write

The archive is organized by intensity instead of topic alone:

- **Light Notes** for quick setup notes, keyboard and laptop details, tools I
  tried, and small lessons.
- **Field Logs** for experience-based records from real work, experiments,
  migrations, deployments, and debugging sessions.
- **Deep Dives** for heavier technical writing, architecture, DevOps, AI agent
  usage, and computer science philosophy.

<Question title="The core question">
  How can I keep a useful history of my own engineering growth without turning
  every post into a formal essay?
</Question>

## How I want the writing flow to work

I want writing here to be close to the way I actually learn. Some posts will be
short and practical. Some will be long and uncertain. Some will be records of
things I only understand after breaking them once.

<Decision title="Current direction">
  I am starting with a small static Astro site, deployed from my homelab and
  wrapped as a Nix flake. The blog itself should be part of the record, not just
  the container for it.
</Decision>

## What belongs here

This blog will likely include:

- homelab and NixOS deployment notes
- DevOps lessons from practical operation
- AI agent workflow experiments
- programming and computer science ideas
- project portfolios and build journals
- CV, profile, timeline, and setup records
- honest notes about tools I use every day

The shape of the site will change as the writing grows. That is intentional. I
want the structure to stay useful, but I do not want the structure to prevent me
from writing.

## Next

The next step is simple: keep publishing small records, then let patterns emerge
from the archive.

<Todo title="First writing habit">
  Publish posts as soon as they preserve a useful piece of experience, even if
  the post is small.
</Todo>
